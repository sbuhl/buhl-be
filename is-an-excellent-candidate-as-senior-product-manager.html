I enumerate 5 things that make me an excellent candidate for the position of Senior Product Manager. Below, in my CV's timeline, you'll discover how each job and life event has shaped those things. Some more than others but each has been a stone in building what I have become.

UX - User Experience Centric
It started as a job. Now it's a passion, my wife call it a distortion. Whatever I'm doing, driving my car, filling a form online, shopping, I'm always thinking, how convenient it'll be if they had move the button here, or this sentence is strange, I would have written blabla, or this design is not good, just change the color to have the text readable. It's now a motto, ease the flow of the user and keep things simple.
UI - Design Fiber
It's an old interest which now helps me in my jobs. Through the few logos, websites, flyers, and other visuals that I created, I gain some skills to know what makes a good design. I believe that it's as much important as the user experience. Actually it helps the user experience. A well balanced design, a good choice in colors and illustration may make the whole difference.
Technical Curiosity
It's always important to understand the complexity of a requested task. Because developers time is valuable and a developement may not worth the technical cost (to create it or maintain the feature over time). But also, it's important to talk the same language and understand each other. That's why I'm keep learning the language but also have a look on the development of almost each task.
Pedagogy Interest
Wheter for adults or children I like to transmit. First, deconstruct the knowledge, and then reconstruct it with stronger basis. Always with a language adapted to the audience. Intelligences are multiple (visual, logical, verbal, etc). And when it comes to onboarding, Product Owners are teachers, responsible for presenting content in a way that engages the most users.
Open Minded and Flexible
Driven by changes, my experiences abroad brough me an open mind. I like to meet people and discover new cultures and ways of thinking. I like to have constant contact with my team of developers and open-minded towards any suggestions from anyone as it may shift the development process for the best.
